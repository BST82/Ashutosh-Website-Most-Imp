Pitra Paksha Shraddh 2025 ‚Äì Dates, Importance, and Rituals

Pitra Paksha, also known as Shraddh Paksha or Mahalaya Paksha, is a highly significant period in Hindu tradition dedicated to honoring our ancestors (Pitru). During these days, Hindus perform Tarpan, Pind Daan, and Shraddh rituals to offer gratitude and seek blessings from their forefathers.

By performing Shraddh in Pitra Paksha, one ensures peace to the departed souls and receives prosperity, happiness, and harmony in the family.

üóì Pitra Paksha 2025 Dates

According to the Hindu Panchang, Pitra Paksha in 2025 will begin on 8th September (Monday) and will end on 17th September (Wednesday) with Sarva Pitru Amavasya.

  • Start Date (Purnima Shraddh): 8th September 2025
  • End Date (Sarva Pitru Amavasya): 17th September 2025

These 15 days are divided according to Tithis where Shraddh rituals are performed for ancestors who departed on that specific lunar day.

üôè Importance of Pitra Paksha

  • It is believed that during this period, the souls of ancestors visit the earth to bless their descendants.
  • Performing Shraddh rituals brings Pitru Kripa (blessings of ancestors) and removes Pitru Dosh.
  • It ensures peace, prosperity, and spiritual upliftment in the family.

üïâ Main Rituals Performed in Pitra Paksha

  • Tarpan: Offering water mixed with sesame seeds and barley to ancestors.
  • Pind Daan: Offering rice balls (Pind) with prayers for peace of the souls.
  • Shraddh Bhoj: Feeding Brahmins, saints, or needy people as an offering to ancestors.
  • Charity (Daan): Donating food, clothes, or other items to the poor in memory of the ancestors.

üå∏ Sarva Pitru Amavasya 2025

The last day of Pitra Paksha, known as Sarva Pitru Amavasya, falls on 17th September 2025. On this day, people who do not know the exact tithi of their ancestors can perform Shraddh and Pind Daan for all their forefathers collectively.
